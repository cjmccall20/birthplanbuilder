# Vitamin K: TL;DR

## The Trade-Off
A single injection provides near-complete protection against a rare but serious bleeding disorder. However, it involves pain during your baby's first hours of life, and some parents prefer oral alternatives or natural vitamin K accumulation.

## What It Is
- Babies are born with low vitamin K (needed for blood clotting)
- VKDB (Vitamin K Deficiency Bleeding) can cause brain hemorrhage and death
- Shot is typically given within first 6 hours of birth
- Oral alternative exists (3 doses over 6 weeks)

## Key Stats
- VKDB risk without vitamin K (breastfed): 4-7 per 100,000 births
- VKDB risk with injection: Less than 0.3 per 100,000 births
- When VKDB occurs: ~50% have brain bleeding; ~50% of those die or have permanent damage
- Higher risk: Exclusively breastfed babies (breast milk is low in vitamin K)

## Options
**Intramuscular shot:** Single dose, ~97% effective, immediate protection, involves one moment of pain
**Oral vitamin K:** 3 doses over 6 weeks, ~80% effective, requires strict compliance, no injection pain

## Cost-Benefit Summary

### With Injection
- Near-complete VKDB protection
- Pain during golden hour
- Polysorbate 80 exposure (within safe limits)
- No follow-up needed

### Without Injection
- No painful procedure in first hours
- Natural vitamin K accumulation
- Real risk of VKDB (rare but catastrophic when it occurs)
- Higher vigilance required

## One Thing to Remember
