# Hepatitis B Vaccine: TL;DR

## Bottom Line
Hep B vaccine at birth is primarily to catch babies of mothers who test falsely negative or have unknown status. If you're confirmed HepB-negative, the 2025 ACIP guidelines now support individual decision-making about timing.

## What It Is
- Hepatitis B is a liver disease spread through blood/body fluids
- Babies infected at birth have 90% chance of chronic infection
- Vaccine is given within 24 hours of birth (standard)

## Key Stats
- Maternal-infant transmission (if mom is HepB+): 40-90%
- Chronic infection if infected at birth: 90%
- US maternal HepB+ rate: <1%
- 2025 policy change: Individual decision for HepB-negative mothers

## The 2025 Change
ACIP now recommends "individual-based decision-making" for birth dose when:
- Mother tested negative during pregnancy
- No household contacts are HepB+
- Vaccine can be given at 2-month visit instead

## Decision Guide
**Birth dose makes sense if:** Unknown maternal status, high-risk household contacts, want to start series early
**Delaying to 2 months may be considered if:** Confirmed maternal negative status, stable family situation, prefer fewer interventions at birth
