# Comprehensive Research on Circumcision

## 1. Religious/Biblical Circumcision vs. Modern Medical Circumcision

### Historical Jewish Practice

The original biblical circumcision, called **milah**, was significantly different from modern circumcision. About four thousand years ago, the procedure consisted of cutting off only the tip of the foreskin—the part that extends past the glans in a normal male infant. This left most of the foreskin intact.

**Sources:**
- [Biblical vs Modern Circumcision - South Florida Mohel](https://southfloridamohel.com/biblical-circumcision-vs-modern-circumcision/)
- [Circumcision Choice - Biblical Circumcision](https://www.circumcisionchoice.com/single-post/biblical)

### The Addition of Periah (2nd Century CE)

A dramatic change occurred in the 2nd century CE when the Tannaim (successors of the Pharisees) introduced and made mandatory a secondary step called **periah**. This procedure involved:

- Tearing and stripping back the remaining inner mucosal lining of the foreskin from the glans
- Using a sharp fingernail or implement to remove all of the inner mucosal tissue
- Excising and removing the frenulum from the underside of the glans
- Ensuring that no part of the remaining penile skin would rest against the glans corona

**Why was periah added?** By the period of the Maccabees, many Jewish men attempted to hide their circumcisions through **epispasm** (foreskin restoration) to assimilate with Greek culture. The rabbis' solution was to "so entirely obliterate the foreskin that any Jew so circumcised would not be able to disguise 'the seal of the covenant.'" According to Dr. Kohler in the Jewish Encyclopedia (1964), "the Rabbis, probably after the war of Bar Kokba, instituted the 'periah' (the laying bare of the glans), without which circumcision was declared to be of no value."

**Sources:**
- [Periah - Mohelis South Florida](http://www.mohelinsouthflorida.com/2018/04/periah.html)
- [Periah - IntactiWiki](https://en.intactiwiki.org/wiki/Periah)
- [Circumcision: a religious obligation or 'the cruellest of cuts'?](https://pmc.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/articles/PMC2801794/)

### Modern Medical Circumcision

When medical doctors started performing circumcision in the 19th century, they adopted the Jewish version with periah. Modern medical circumcision removes the entire sleeve of skin and mucosal tissue that normally covers the glans—far more tissue than biblical circumcision.

**Critical distinction:** "It is interesting to note, from an historical point of view, that by these calculations all biblical Jews—both Old and New Testament—would have been circumcised in the less radical, symbolic style of milah. This being the case, no biblical reference to circumcision ever refers to or indicates the more radical style of circumcision which is now practiced by modern-day Jews or by the American medical profession."

**Sources:**
- [Fish Eaters - Biblical vs Modern Circumcision](https://www.fisheaters.com/circumcision2.html)
- [Male Circumcision: Integrating Tradition and Medical Evidence](https://pmc.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/articles/PMC3638798/)

## 2. Origins of Routine Circumcision in the United States

### Pre-19th Century

Until 1870, circumcision was a cultural practice limited primarily to Jews and Muslims. For most of its existence, the United States, with its overwhelmingly Protestant population of Northern-European descent, had no tradition or history of circumcision.

**Source:** [History of circumcision - Wikipedia](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/History_of_circumcision)

### Late 19th Century: The Anti-Masturbation Movement

In the late 19th century, circumcision ceased to be the preserve of Jews and Muslims and was transformed into a supposed medical necessity. Victorian-era doctors in the English-speaking world began promoting circumcision as a "cure" for masturbation, which was thought to cause insanity, epilepsy, blindness, and other grave ailments.

**Dr. Lewis Sayre (1870s)** was a key figure in promoting circumcision. He believed circumcision could cure neuromuscular disease and argued for its use on every male child. He postulated the concept of "reflex genital irritation," viewing the foreskin as a danger to the entire organism. He believed circumcision could transform a child who "was like a lunatic" to one capable of docility and civilization.

**Technological developments:**
- William Stewart Halsted's 1885 discovery of hypodermic cocaine as a local anesthetic made minor surgeries easier
- Several mechanically aided circumcision techniques (forerunners of modern clamps) were published in the 1890s
- The germ theory of disease led doctors to view smegma as infectious material

**Sources:**
- [The Surprising History of Circumcision in the US: A Timeline](https://scientifically.blog/history-circumcision-us-timeline)
- [Circumcision Revisited: An Historical Perspective](https://www.urologicalcare.com/circumcision-revisited-an-historical-perspective/)

### John Harvey Kellogg's Role

John Harvey Kellogg (1852-1943), inventor of Corn Flakes and director of the Battle Creek Sanitarium, was a prominent figure in the anti-masturbation movement. However, his actual influence on routine circumcision is often misunderstood:

**What Kellogg actually advocated:**
- He recommended circumcision **without anesthetic** specifically for boys who were caught masturbating, believing the trauma and weeks of pain would curb the practice
- He **never promoted routine circumcision of all boys** and had no interest in performing the procedure on infants
- He endorsed it solely for the purpose of inflicting genital pain as punishment
- For girls, he recommended applying carbolic acid to the clitoris

**His broader philosophy:** Kellogg followed the teachings of Ellen G. White and Sylvester Graham, who recommended a diet of bland foods to minimize excitement, sexual arousal, and masturbation. His corn flakes were designed as part of this bland diet.

**Historical debate:** While medical historians argue that stopping masturbation was a main reason circumcision became routine in the 19th century, there is debate about Kellogg's actual influence. Common misconception credits Kellogg with instigating routine infant circumcision, but "the true surge in routine neonatal circumcision, transforming it from a niche practice into a pervasive American standard, would manifest dramatically in the following century."

**Sources:**
- [John Harvey Kellogg - Wikipedia](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/John_Harvey_Kellogg)
- [Kellogg's Crusade - Circumcision Choice](https://www.circumcisionchoice.com/single-post/kellogg)
- [John Harvey Kellogg And His Anti-Masturbation Cereals](https://arenaissancewriter.medium.com/john-harvey-kellogg-and-his-anti-masturbation-cereals-832440cd0f5b)

### Early to Mid-20th Century: Becoming Routine

The early to mid-20th century witnessed a rapid shift in neonatal circumcision prevalence:

- **Medical justifications** in the 20th century linked circumcision with hygiene, cancer prevention, and increased sexual functioning
- **Military influence** during World Wars spread the practice
- **Medicalization of childbirth** made circumcision a sign of hospital delivery by a doctor (rather than home delivery by a midwife)
- **Cold War era**: A program of universal, neonatal circumcision was instituted in many American hospitals
- **Standard practice**: Until recently, it was routinely performed unless parents specifically requested otherwise

By 1996, no less than 60% of all US male infants were circumcised—far higher than the rest of the industrialized world.

**Sources:**
- [The Circumcision Debate: A brief history](http://circumcisiondebate.org/past-and-present)
- [American Circumcision Practices and Social Reality](https://www.bjpa.org/content/upload/bjpa/circ/Circumcision.pdf)

### Late 20th Century: Beginning to Question

By the early 1970s, the medical justifications for routine circumcision began to erode and were challenged. Circumcision rates have declined in recent decades as medical understanding evolved.

**Source:** [History of circumcision - Wikipedia](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/History_of_circumcision)

## 3. Functions of the Foreskin

### Anatomy and Structure

The foreskin (prepuce) is a double-layered fold of skin, mucosal and muscular tissue at the distal end of the penis that covers the glans and urinary meatus. It is mobile, fairly stretchable, and keeps the glans in a moist environment. The foreskin is attached to the glans by an elastic band of tissue called the **frenulum**.

**Key anatomical components:**
- Outer skin layer
- Inner mucosal skin
- Ridged band
- Frenulum
- Dartos fascia

**Source:** [Foreskin - Wikipedia](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Foreskin)

### The Ridged Band

John R. Taylor, MB, a Canadian pathologist, first described the **ridged band** in 1991 after examining foreskins of 22 adults obtained at autopsy. The ridged band:

- Encircles the inner layer of the foreskin with 12-14 ridges
- Contains tens of thousands of specialized erogenous nerves
- Houses Meissner's corpuscles (fine touch nerve endings)
- Is proposed to be the primary sensory tissue of the penis

**Function:** Taylor theorizes that stretching of the accordion-like ridged band triggers important sexual reflexes and erogenous sensation. The ridged band transmits impulses to the spinal cord, which in turn transmits impulses to the penis and the muscle responsible for ejaculation—making it both touch-sensitive and reflexogenic.

**Sources:**
- [Do You Know: About the Ridged Band and Frenulum?](https://intactamerica.org/do-you-know-about-the-ridged-band-and-frenulum/)
- [The Importance of the Foreskin to Male Sexual Reflexes](https://link.springer.com/chapter/10.1007/978-1-4757-4011-0_3)

### The Frenulum

The frenulum is a strong, elastic band of tissue connecting the foreskin to the glans on the underside of the penis. It:

- Helps stabilize the movement of the foreskin and keep it in place
- Contains numerous nerve endings that contribute to sexual pleasure
- Along with the frenular delta, is one of the most sensitive parts of the penis after the ridged band
- May trigger ejaculation through stretching during the rearward gliding action of penetration

**Sources:**
- [Penis Frenulum: Location, Function & Conditions](https://my.clevelandclinic.org/health/body/23533-penis-frenulum)
- [What is Foreskin | Anatomy and Function](https://intaction.org/foreskin/)

### Nerve Endings and Sensitivity

The foreskin contains:
- **Meissner's corpuscles** - specialized sensory nerve endings involved in fine-touch sensitivity
- **Somatosensory innervation** from the dorsal nerve of the penis and perineal nerve branches
- Both nociceptors and mechanoreceptors

**Clarifying the "20,000 nerve endings" claim:** This number is a myth with no credible scientific source. Estimates suggest the foreskin contains somewhere between **1,000 and 10,000 nerve endings** (Ken McGrath, pathologist).

**Sources:**
- [Histological Correlates of Penile Sexual Sensation](https://pmc.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/articles/PMC4498824/)
- [How Many Nerve Endings In The Foreskin? Definitely Not 20,000](https://www.allohealth.com/blog/sexual-education/anatomy/how-many-nerve-endings-in-the-foreskin)
- [Vital or vestigial? The foreskin has its fans and foes](https://pmc.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/articles/PMC3225416/)

### Protective Function

In 1949, British physician Douglas Gairdner noted: "During the years when the child is incontinent the glans is completely clothed by the prepuce, for, deprived of this protection, the glans becomes susceptible to injury from contact with sodden clothes or napkin."

**Protective mechanisms:**
- Fully covers and protects the glans from harsh rubbing against clothing and damage
- Maintains sensitivity by keeping the glans as moist mucous membrane
- Without the foreskin, the glans skin becomes dry and thickens considerably (keratinization), reducing sensitivity

**Sources:**
- [Male Foreskin is Functional – 5 Amazing Facts](https://15square.org.uk/male-foreskin-is-functional-5-amazing-facts/)
- [Functions of the Foreskin](https://circumcision.org/functions-of-the-foreskin/)

### Sexual Function

- **Reduces friction** during sex, reducing the need for additional lubrication
- **Gliding mechanism** facilitates the necessary expansion of skin on the penile shaft and glans
- **Provides natural sensations** and retains lubricating fluids during intercourse
- **Stimulates partner** through the gliding action

**The College of Physicians and Surgeons of British Columbia** wrote that the foreskin is "composed of an outer skin and an inner mucosa that is rich in specialized sensory nerve endings and erogenous tissue."

**Sources:**
- [The Human Foreskin](https://www.foregen.org/the-human-foreskin)
- [Foreskin - Wikipedia](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Foreskin)

### Immunological Function

The foreskin provides immunological functions, though research in this area continues. The mucous membrane contains immune cells and antibacterial properties.

**Source:** [The Human Foreskin](https://www.foregen.org/the-human-foreskin)

## 4. Sexual Satisfaction Research

### Systematic Reviews of the Literature

**Large 2013 systematic review** by Morris & Krieger:
- Analyzed 2,675 publications, 36 met inclusion criteria
- Total of 40,473 men (19,542 uncircumcised, 20,931 circumcised)
- Included 2 high-quality randomized controlled trials (rated 1++)
- **Findings:** Studies uniformly found that circumcision had **no overall adverse effect** on penile sensitivity, sexual arousal, sexual sensation, erectile function, premature ejaculation, ejaculatory latency, orgasm difficulties, sexual satisfaction, pleasure, or pain during penetration
- **Conclusion:** "The consensus of the highest quality literature is that MC has minimal or no adverse effect, and in some studies, it has benefits on sexual functions, sensation, satisfaction, and pleasure for males circumcised neonatally or in adulthood."

**Source:** [Does male circumcision affect sexual function, sensitivity, or satisfaction?--a systematic review](https://pubmed.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/23937309/)

### Studies on Men Circumcised as Adults

These studies are particularly valuable because men serve as their own control, comparing before and after experiences.

**Kenya Randomized Controlled Trial (Bailey et al., 2007):**
- Over 2,700 adult men
- No loss of sexual function reported
- Many noted improvements in hygiene and sensitivity
- Circumcised men reported **increased penile sensitivity** and **enhanced ease of reaching orgasm**
- **Conclusion:** "These data indicate that integration of male circumcision into programs to reduce HIV risk is unlikely to adversely affect male sexual function."

**Rakai, Uganda Trial:**
- No statistically significant differences in satisfaction between circumcised and uncircumcised men (98.4% vs 99.9%)
- No significant difference in medium to high level of sexual desire (99.1% vs 99.3%)

**Turkish Study on Adult Circumcision:**
- Significant improvements in sexual life and confidence scores
- When evaluated with premature ejaculation effects, circumcision appeared to improve sexual satisfaction
- Note: "Conflicting results among the studies for the effect of circumcision on sexual life may be explained by cultural differences."

**Sources:**
- [Adult Male Circumcision: Effects on Sexual Function and Sexual Satisfaction in Kisumu, Kenya](https://pmc.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/articles/PMC3042320/)
- [Psychological and sexual effects of circumcision in adult males](https://pmc.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/articles/PMC5065403/)

### Contrasting Evidence

**2013 Study (Bronselaer et al.):**
- Sample: 1,059 uncircumcised and 310 circumcised men
- **For the glans penis:** Circumcised men reported decreased sexual pleasure and lower orgasm intensity, more effort required to achieve orgasm, and higher percentage experienced unusual sensations (burning, prickling, itching, tingling, numbness)
- **For the penile shaft:** Higher percentage of circumcised men described discomfort, pain, numbness, and unusual sensations
- Men circumcised during adolescence or later indicated **less sexual pleasure** compared to those circumcised before puberty

**2013 Study (Sorrells et al.):**
- Male circumcision decreases penile sensitivity in a large cohort
- Found reduced fine-touch sensitivity in circumcised men

**Sources:**
- [Male circumcision decreases penile sensitivity as measured in a large cohort](https://pubmed.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/23374102/)
- [The Contrasting Evidence Concerning the Effect of Male Circumcision on Sexual Function, Sensation, and Pleasure: A Systematic Review](https://pmc.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/articles/PMC7691872/)

### Important Considerations and Conflicts

**Study limitations:**
- Some critics suggest studies of men circumcised as adults and for therapeutic reasons should have been excluded because "such men had a motive to get circumcised"
- Some systematic review authors have declared conflicts of interest, including being members of circumcision advocacy organizations and coinventors of circumcision devices

**Institutional positions:**
- In 2009, the World Health Organization called it a "myth" that circumcision has an effect on sexual pleasure
- This view is echoed by other major medical organizations
- However, some researchers argue the loss of specialized nerve endings may lead to reduced sensitivity over time

**Sources:**
- [Does Circumcision Affect Sex? What Science Shows](https://www.safecirc.com/education-and-articles/does-circumcision-affect-sex-what-the-evidence-says)
- [Contrasting Evidence Concerning the Effect of Male Circumcision on Sexual Function, Sensation, and Pleasure: A Systematic Review](https://academic.oup.com/smoa/article/8/4/577/6956606)

## 5. Circumcision Deaths and Complications

### Estimated Annual Deaths in the United States

**Bollinger Study:**
- Estimates **more than 100 neonatal circumcision-related deaths (9.01/100,000)** occur annually in the United States
- Approximately **117 neonatal deaths** due directly or indirectly to circumcision per year
- **One out of every 77 male neonatal deaths** (1.3% of male neonatal deaths from all causes)
- Comparison: 44 neonatal deaths from suffocation, 8 in automobile accidents, 115 from SIDS
- **Because infant circumcision is elective, all of these deaths are avoidable**

**Note:** Previous studies have estimated death rates ranging from as low as 2 per year to as many as 230. The wide range reflects challenges in data collection.

**Sources:**
- [100+ circumcision deaths each year in United States](https://bestdoulas.com/wp-content/uploads/2020/06/circdeathrisk.pdf)
- [Lost Boys: An Estimate of U.S. Circumcision-Related Infant Deaths](https://www.researchgate.net/publication/240804903_Lost_Boys_An_Estimate_of_US_Circumcision-Related_Infant_Deaths)
- [Newborn circumcision linked to 100 deaths in US each year](https://intactamerica.org/newborn-circumcision-linked-to-100-deaths-in-us-each-year/)

### National Inpatient Sample Data (2001-2010)

A peer-reviewed study in Clinical Pediatrics found:
- Over 10 years, **200 early deaths** were recorded among 9,833,110 subjects
- **1 death per 49,166 circumcisions**
- Note: This should be interpreted as correlational, not necessarily causal, and may include both undercounting and overcounting
- Compared with survivors, infants who died were more likely to have comorbid conditions (cardiac disease, coagulopathy, fluid/electrolyte disorders, pulmonary circulatory disorders)

**Source:** [Factors Associated With Early Deaths Following Neonatal Male Circumcision in the United States, 2001 to 2010](https://pubmed.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/30066572/)

### Causes of Death

Deaths from circumcision complications include:
- Anesthesia reaction
- Stroke
- Hemorrhage
- Infection
- Cardiac arrest

**Data collection challenges:** Many circumcision-related deaths are reported as surgical mishap, infection, hemorrhage, cardiac arrest, stroke, or reaction to anesthesia rather than being directly attributed to the circumcision procedure. "Because of the inadequacies of the death-certificate system and the apparent lack of investigation, it is easy to see how the medical system could either unwittingly or intentionally obscure the true cause of these deaths."

**Sources:**
- [Circumcision deaths in the USA - Darbon Institute](https://www.darboninstitute.org/circumcision_deaths_complications_usa)
- [Serious and Fatal Complications after Neonatal Circumcision](https://pubmed.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/34973956/)

### Criticism of Higher Estimates

Some sources claim the Bollinger study "has been shown to have no credibility, as it was based on an assumption that the difference in annual death rate between male and female infants was entirely due to circumcision-related deaths." Critics note that the sex difference in infant mortality is similar for countries having both low and high infant circumcision rates.

**Source:** [Circumcision deaths in USA | Circinfo.org](https://www.circinfo.org/USA_deaths.html)

### Complication Rates

**United States - Standard Claims:**
- Procedure-related complication rates in infants are low, ranging from **0.2% to 0.6%**
- Acute complications include:
  - Bleeding: 0.08% to 0.18%
  - Injury to the penis: 0.04%
  - Infection: 0.06%

**Large U.S. Study (2010-2022):**
- **30-day complication rate: 0.96%** of newborns
- 2-year and 5-year secondary procedure rates decreased from 2.90% and 3.62% in 2010 to 2.45% in 2020 and 2.89% in 2017

**Sources:**
- [Characterization of Newborn Circumcision Revision and Complication Rates Among Privately Insured Boys in the United States Between 2010 and 2022](https://pubmed.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/39970967/)
- [Evidence and Ethics on: Circumcision](https://evidencebasedbirth.com/evidence-and-ethics-on-circumcision/)

### Revision Surgery and Botched Circumcisions

**Community hospital study:**
- 1.4% referred to urology for potential complications
- **0.3% required revision surgery**

**In the United States:**
- Prevalence of complications requiring surgical revision: **0.4%**
- Most common complication: not enough foreskin removed, leading to revision requests
- Reported surgical complication rate: **2-3%**, most are minor post-operative bleeding

**Massachusetts General Hospital retrospective review (476 late complications):**
- 40% for inadequate circumcision
- 5% for phimosis requiring revision

**Overall complication range:** 0%–16% (median 1.5%)
- Main complications: bleeding, glans injury, infection, adhesion, excessive foreskin removal, meatal stenosis, phimosis, false micropenis

**Sources:**
- [Complications Associated With Circumcision in a Community-Based Hospital](https://pmc.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/articles/PMC12303146/)
- [A comprehensive comparison of the early and late complications of surgical circumcision](https://pmc.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/articles/PMC9679242/)
- [Circumcision | Boston Children's Hospital](https://www.childrenshospital.org/treatments/circumcision)

### International Data - Ghana Study

Complication rates varied dramatically by provider:
- **Doctors: 4.3%**
- **Nurses: 39.1%**
- **Traditional circumcisers: 34.8%**

**Salvage surgeries for circumcision mishaps:**
- Circumcision-revision surgery: 31.82% (most common)
- Urethroplasty and hypospadias repair: 15.91%
- Success rate for salvage surgeries: 70%

**Source:** [Clinical outcomes of circumcisions and prevalence of complications of male circumcisions: Ghana](https://journals.plos.org/globalpublichealth/article?id=10.1371/journal.pgph.0003788)

### Provider Impact on Outcomes

Compared to circumcisions performed by obstetricians/gynecologists, the odds of revision within 2 years were lower for:
- Pediatricians: 0.46
- Urologists: 0.29
- Pediatric urologists: 0.38
- Pediatric surgeons: 0.60

**Source:** [Characterization of Newborn Circumcision Revision and Complication Rates](https://pubmed.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/39970967/)

## 6. Global Circumcision Statistics

### Worldwide Prevalence

Global prevalence of male circumcision is estimated at **37-39%**, with most estimates around **38%**. About **one-third of males worldwide** are circumcised, though prevalence varies significantly by country and culture.

**Source:** [Estimation of country-specific and global prevalence of male circumcision](https://pmc.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/articles/PMC4772313/)

### Regional Breakdown

**Highest Rates:**
- **North Africa and Middle East: 99%** of men aged 15 and older
- Countries like Morocco, Palestine, and Afghanistan: virtually **100%**
- **12 countries** have circumcision rates higher than 99%
- **More than 50 countries** have rates above 90% but below 99%
- **Africa overall:** About 62% of African males are circumcised

**Western Countries (Prevalence Ranking):**
1. United States: **71-80%** (estimates vary; 80% between 2008-2021)
2. New Zealand: 33%
3. Australia: 27%
4. United Kingdom: 21%
5. France: 14%
6. Germany: 11%
7. Sweden: 5%
8. Italy: 3%
9. Ireland: 1%

**Lowest Rates:**
- Circumcision is rare in Europe, Latin America, and most of Asia
- South America and Europe have very low rates
- Parts of Asia where Islam is not a majority religion

**Sources:**
- [Circumcision by Country 2026](https://worldpopulationreview.com/country-rankings/circumcision-by-country)
- [Male circumcision: 1 in 3 globally but almost universal in Muslim and Jewish countries](https://www.sydney.edu.au/news-opinion/news/2016/03/08/male-circumcision--1-in-3-globally-but-almost-universal-in-musli.html)

### Key Factors Influencing Rates

**Religious and cultural:**
- Most common among Muslims and Jews (part of religious law in Judaism, established practice in Islam)
- Around **50% of all circumcisions worldwide** are performed for preventive healthcare reasons
- The other **50%** are performed for religious and cultural reasons

**Trends:**
- Australia, Canada, Ireland, New Zealand, and United Kingdom have seen **declines** in recent decades
- Indications of **increasing demand in southern Africa**, partly for preventive reasons due to the HIV epidemic
- United States remains an outlier with much higher rates than other Western countries

## 7. Circumcision-Tylenol-Autism Hypothesis

### Overview of the Hypothesis

In October 2025, U.S. Health Secretary Robert F. Kennedy Jr. claimed: "There's two studies that show children who are circumcised early have double the rate of autism. It's highly likely because they are given Tylenol."

**The proposed mechanism:**
1. Boys are more likely to be circumcised than girls
2. Circumcised boys receive Tylenol (acetaminophen) for pain
3. Tylenol exposure correlates with autism
4. Boys have higher rates of autism than girls
5. Therefore, circumcision → Tylenol → autism connection explains higher autism in boys

**Source:** [RFK, Jr., Cites 'Truly Appalling' Studies to Tie Autism to Circumcision and Tylenol](https://www.scientificamerican.com/article/rfk-jr-cites-truly-appalling-studies-to-tie-autism-to-circumcision-and/)

### The Two Studies Referenced

**1. Danish Study (2015) - Frisch & Simonsen:**
- National, register-based cohort study of 342,877 boys born 1994-2003
- Followed from ages 0-9 years
- Total of 4,986 ASD cases
- **Findings:** Circumcised boys were more likely to develop ASD before age 10 (HR = 1.46; 95% CI: 1.11–1.93)
- Risk was particularly high for infantile autism before age 5 (HR = 2.06; 95% CI: 1.36–3.13)
- **Important limitation:** Authors stated they had **no data on painkillers or anesthetics used** and were "unable to address the paracetamol (or acetaminophen) hypothesis directly"
- The study hypothesized that **pain from circumcision**, not necessarily Tylenol, might impact ASD risk

**Source:** [Ritual circumcision and risk of autism spectrum disorder in 0- to 9-year-old boys: national cohort study in Denmark](https://pmc.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/articles/PMC4530408/)

**2. UMass-Lowell Study (2013) - Bauer & Krieger:**
- Aimed to see if association exists between giving infants acetaminophen and developing autism
- Described by authors as "hypothesis generating exploratory analysis"
- **Circumcision was not the focus** - data about the procedure was analyzed as if it were a proxy for giving Tylenol
- **Critical limitation:** The study **did not confirm whether Tylenol was actually given** in the cases cited
- This was an ecological/country-level study, not individual-level data

**Source:** [RFK Jr.'s Inaccurate Claims About Tylenol, Circumcision and Autism](https://www.factcheck.org/2025/10/rfk-jr-s-inaccurate-claims-about-tylenol-circumcision-and-autism/)

### Scientific Criticism of the Hypothesis

**Study limitations and methodological concerns:**

1. **No actual Tylenol data:** "This study didn't examine use of acetaminophen at all. Their hypothesis is that the pain associated with ritual circumcision causes autism. RFK is making a leap unsupported by their analysis." - Dr. David Mandell

2. **Sample size issues:** One ecological study looked at association involving **only 8 countries**, without controlling for:
   - Average age of parents
   - Genetics
   - Awareness of autism
   - Diagnostic practices
   - All of these factors could explain correlation

   "They don't take into consideration any other factors about those countries." - Dr. Helen Tager-Flusberg

3. **Both studies found some association, but:**
   - Small sample sizes
   - Significant methodological limitations
   - Neither showed circumcision **causes** autism
   - Neither had data on actual Tylenol use

**Sources:**
- [RFK Jr. suggests circumcision is linked to autism. Here's what experts say.](https://www.cbsnews.com/news/rfk-jr-circumcision-linked-autism-experts/)
- [Scientists weigh in on autism, Tylenol and circumcision claims](https://www.washingtonpost.com/health/2025/10/10/circumcision-tylenol-autism-rfk/)

### Timing Doesn't Add Up

**Clinical practice contradicts the theory:**
- According to the American Academy of Pediatrics, **children younger than 12 weeks should not be given acetaminophen** unless instructed by their pediatrician
- Most circumcisions happen **before 12 weeks** with a local anesthetic
- "We almost never, ever use Tylenol after circumcision." - Dr. Steven Abelowitz (performed ~10,000 circumcisions)

**Source:** [Reupping unproven claims about Tylenol, Kennedy claims a link between circumcision and autism](https://www.pbs.org/newshour/health/reupping-unproven-claims-about-tylenol-kennedy-claims-a-link-between-circumcision-and-autism)

### Current Scientific Consensus

**Expert statements:**
- "The conclusion by any credible medical resource is agreeing that there's **no causal relationship** between Tylenol, circumcisions or vaccines to autism." - Dr. Steven Abelowitz
- After reviewing both studies, scientists found **no evidence supporting the claim** that circumcision leads to autism or any other adverse psychological effects
- Decades of research shows acetaminophen is **safe for infants and children** when used as recommended under pediatrician guidance
- There is **very little evidence** that giving acetaminophen to babies/children increases autism risk
- There is **very little evidence** that acetaminophen during pregnancy increases autism risk

**In the decade-plus since publication:** Autism researchers have heavily criticized these studies. The hypothesis lacks support from the broader scientific community.

**Sources:**

## 8. Vitamin K and Circumcision Connection

### Why Vitamin K is Important for Newborns

Vitamin K is a nutrient that helps blood clot and stop bleeding. Newborns have:
- Low levels of vitamin K in their blood at birth
- Limited transplacental transfer during pregnancy
- Minimal vitamin K reserves in the liver
- A sterile gut unable to synthesize vitamin K
- This makes newborns uniquely vulnerable to hemorrhagic disorders

**Vitamin K is crucial for clotting:** Prothrombin and three other pro-clotting proteins (factors II, VII, IX, and X) depend on vitamin K for synthesis.

**Sources:**
- [Vitamin K and the Newborn Infant](https://publications.aap.org/pediatrics/article/149/3/e2021056036/184866/Vitamin-K-and-the-Newborn-Infant)
- [Common questions about vitamin K](https://www.mayoclinichealthsystem.org/hometown-health/speaking-of-health/common-questions-about-vitamin-k)

### Connection to Circumcision and Bleeding Risk

**Before vitamin K prophylaxis became standard:**
- **One out of every nine baby boys** who had elective newborn circumcision without vitamin K had bleeding that wouldn't stop
- Infant males had significantly increased risk of bleeding during circumcision

**After vitamin K became standard (1961):**
- The risk of bleeding during circumcision has significantly decreased
- IM vitamin K is the most effective prophylaxis against VKDB, **reducing bleeding risk up to sixfold** in neonatal circumcision

**Timing considerations:**
- Newborn circumcision is typically performed within the first 10 days, often within the first 48 hours
- Prothrombin (PT) levels fluctuate significantly in the first week:
  - PT levels are high at birth
  - Drop within 24 hours
  - Reach a **nadir (lowest point) at 24-72 hours**
  - Then rebound
- Classic VKDB occurs on days 2-7 of life, when vitamin K levels are naturally at their lowest
- Common bleeding sites include gastrointestinal system, umbilical cord, skin, nose, and **circumcision site**

**Sources:**
- [Is a Vitamin K Shot Required for Circumcision?](https://thenewborncircumcisioncenter.com/circumcision-faqs/vitamin-k-shot-required-for-circumcision/)
- [Vitamin K Deficiency Bleeding and Early Infant Male Circumcision in Africa](https://pmc.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/articles/PMC3773869/)
- [Evidence on: The Vitamin K Shot in Newborns](https://evidencebasedbirth.com/evidence-for-the-vitamin-k-shot-in-newborns/)

### Case Study Evidence

During a circumcision trial, one infant bled for 90 minutes post-procedure. After discovering he had not received standard prophylactic vitamin K, **2 mg phytomenadione (vitamin K1) was given intramuscularly; bleeding stopped within 30 minutes**. This demonstrates vitamin K's extremely rapid action.

**Source:** [Circumcision without vitamin K: Why most hospitals say no](https://www.safecirc.com/education-and-articles/circumcision-without-vitamin-k-why-most-hospitals-say-no-and-how-safecirc-says-yes-safely)

### When Parents Decline Vitamin K

**Hospital policy:** Because circumcision carries a real risk of bleeding, **most hospitals will refuse to perform the procedure if the infant has not received IM vitamin K**.

**Alternative approaches when parents refuse:**
- Some research suggests timing circumcisions **after 14 days** (after PT stabilization) may be safer
- Circumcisions performed at 14 days on 10 patients without IM vitamin K showed no bleeding complications
- However, this timing is not standard practice

**Source:** [Neonatal circumcisions and parental refusal of intramuscular vitamin K: A review](https://pubmed.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/39743430/)

### Is Vitamin K Shot Primarily for Circumcision?

**No - Vitamin K has broader critical purposes:**

Since 1961, the American Academy of Pediatrics has recommended a single dose of vitamin K injected into the thigh muscle of **all newborns** to prevent **Vitamin K Deficiency Bleeding (VKDB)**, also known as Hemorrhagic Disease of the Newborn.

**Types of VKDB:**
1. **Early VKDB** (within 24 hours): Usually linked to maternal medications
2. **Classic VKDB** (days 2-7): More common in breastfed babies; bleeding sites include GI system, umbilical cord, skin, nose, circumcision site
3. **Late VKDB** (2-12 weeks): Rare but serious, often involves brain bleeds

**Breast milk is particularly deficient in vitamin K**, making prophylaxis at birth critical for preventing the disease.

**VKDB is life-threatening:**
- Associated with high mortality and morbidity
- One of the most important causes of **intracranial hemorrhage** in the first year of life
- **Easily preventable** by administering 1 mg IM vitamin K within 1 hour of birth

**Sources:**
- [Hemorrhagic Disease of Newborn](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/books/NBK558994/)
- [Vitamin K Deficiency Bleeding (Hemorrhagic Disease of the Newborn)](https://www.chop.edu/conditions-diseases/vitamin-k-deficiency-bleeding-hemorrhagic-disease-newborn)
- [Vitamin K | Newborn Nursery | Stanford Medicine](https://med.stanford.edu/newborns/clinical-guidelines/vitamink.html)

### Would Declining Circumcision Change the Vitamin K Calculus?

**No - The recommendation would remain unchanged:**

Vitamin K prophylaxis is recommended for **all newborns**, regardless of circumcision status, because:

1. **Classic VKDB affects all infants**, with common bleeding sites including:
   - Gastrointestinal system
   - Umbilical cord site
   - Skin
   - Nose
   - As well as circumcision site (when applicable)

2. **Late VKDB** (2-12 weeks) is rare but serious, often involving **brain bleeds** - this can occur regardless of circumcision

3. **Systematic reviews confirm:** Single IM injection of vitamin K at birth effectively prevents VKDB, with **no failures reported with IM prophylaxis**

4. **Rising cases:** Despite the success of prevention, VKDB incidence appears to be on the rise, attributable to parental refusal and lowered efficacy of alternative administration methods (oral dosing)

5. **Before universal prophylaxis:** VKDB caused significant mortality and morbidity from bleeding unrelated to circumcision

**Intramuscular administration is the gold standard:**
- Oral vitamin K is less effective than IM vitamin K
- The FDA has **not approved an oral vitamin K for newborns**
- IM vitamin K should remain standard care for all infants

**Sources:**
- [Prophylactic vitamin K for vitamin K deficiency bleeding in neonates](https://pmc.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/articles/PMC6718236/)
- [Protect Your Baby from Bleeds | Vitamin K Deficiency Bleeding | CDC](https://www.cdc.gov/vitamin-k-deficiency/fact-sheet/index.html)
- [Guidelines for vitamin K prophylaxis in newborns | Canadian Paediatric Society](https://cps.ca/en/documents/position/vitamin-k-prophylaxis-in-newborns)

### Historical Context

Before 1961 (when vitamin K became standard), circumcision did contribute to bleeding complications. However, the establishment of universal vitamin K prophylaxis was driven by broader concerns about hemorrhagic disease of the newborn affecting **all infants**, not specifically to enable circumcision. The fact that it dramatically reduced circumcision bleeding was an additional benefit, not the primary rationale.

**Source:** [Vitamin K, Circumcision, and the Eighth Day](https://thechicagomohel.com/vitamin-k-circumcision/)

## Conclusion

This research reveals several key findings:

1. **Biblical vs. Modern Circumcision:** Modern medical circumcision removes far more tissue than the original biblical practice. The addition of periah in the 2nd century CE transformed circumcision from removing just the tip to complete removal of the foreskin.

2. **U.S. History:** Routine circumcision in the U.S. originated in the Victorian era's anti-masturbation movement and became standard through a combination of medical justifications, military influence, and the medicalization of childbirth—not solely through Kellogg's influence.

3. **Foreskin Functions:** The foreskin serves multiple functions including protection, sensation (through the ridged band and frenulum with thousands of specialized nerve endings), sexual function (gliding mechanism), and immunological functions.

4. **Sexual Satisfaction:** Research is mixed. High-quality studies, including those of men circumcised as adults, often find no adverse effect or even improvements, but some studies report decreased sensitivity and sexual pleasure. Conflicts of interest and study design limitations complicate interpretation.

5. **Deaths and Complications:** Estimates of annual circumcision-related deaths in the U.S. range from 2 to 230, with one prominent study estimating 117 deaths per year. Complication rates are generally reported as low (0.2-0.6%), though some studies show higher rates. Revision surgery is needed in 0.3-0.4% of cases.

6. **Global Statistics:** About 38% of males worldwide are circumcised. The practice is nearly universal in Muslim and Jewish populations but rare in most of Europe, Latin America, and Asia. The U.S. has exceptionally high rates (71-80%) compared to other Western nations.

7. **Circumcision-Tylenol-Autism:** The hypothesis lacks scientific support. The two studies cited did not have data on actual Tylenol use, had significant methodological limitations, and experts broadly reject the claimed causal connection.

8. **Vitamin K:** While vitamin K prophylaxis significantly reduces circumcision bleeding risk, it is recommended for all newborns to prevent hemorrhagic disease regardless of circumcision status. The vitamin K shot was not instituted primarily for circumcision safety but for broader protection against VKDB in all infants.

This research aims to provide parents with comprehensive, evidence-based information to make informed decisions about circumcision in accordance with their values and beliefs.
