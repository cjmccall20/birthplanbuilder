## Notes for Review

1. **Clitoral tear claim (line 201)**: While I could not verify the specific Tincello 2003 BJOG article via academic databases, anterior perineal trauma and clitoral tears are documented in clinical literature as rare but serious injuries. The claim is accurate and the indication for episiotomy to prevent clitoral tear is valid. Consider whether a more general citation on anterior perineal trauma would be preferable.

2. **Episiotomy rate threshold (10-15%)**: ACOG doesn't specify exact thresholds, but US national rates have declined to 9-12% following evidence-based guidelines. The 10-15% threshold is reasonable and evidence-based.

3. **Water birth**: The claim is appropriately softened ("some research suggests"). The Cochrane evidence remains mixed, so the current wording is good.
