## RECOMMENDATIONS FOR CHAPTER AUTHORS

### Best Practices Going Forward

1. **Always Use DOIs**
   - Include DOI for every academic citation
   - Enables reader verification
   - Ensures source accessibility

2. **In-Text Citation Markers**
   - Every factual claim needs a [#] marker
   - Don't rely on context - be explicit
   - Include page numbers for books

3. **Verify Before Claiming Specifics**
   - Don't cite "2024 study" unless you have the source
   - Don't claim specific journal/year unless verified
   - Better to say "recent research suggests" than cite wrong year

4. **Use Primary Sources**
   - CDC for government statistics
   - ACOG for professional guidelines
   - Cochrane for systematic reviews
   - Original research papers for specific claims

5. **Keep Cost Data Current**
   - Review all dollar amounts annually
   - Note year of cost data in text
   - Use ranges rather than specific figures when uncertain

6. **Distinguish Fact from Editorial**
   - Editorial guidance doesn't need citations
   - Factual claims always need sources
   - Value statements and recommendations can be uncited if framed as author perspective
