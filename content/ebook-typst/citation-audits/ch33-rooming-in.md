### CLAIM 2: Most hospitals now default to rooming-in
**Location:** Line 9, "WHAT IT IS"
**Text:** "Most hospitals now default to this."

**Status:** ⚠️ PARTIALLY VERIFIED

**Verified Source:**
- **Web research:** Baby-Friendly USA statistics

**Key findings:**
- In 2007, less than 3% of US births occurred in approximately 60 Baby-Friendly facilities
- By recent years, approximately **25% of births** occur in roughly 600 Baby-Friendly facilities
- CDC reports that rooming-in is the norm in **less than half** of all US maternity hospitals

**Verification notes:** The claim "most hospitals" is not fully supported by current data. Rooming-in is practiced in less than half of US hospitals, though it is increasingly common.

**RECOMMENDATION:** Soften to "Many hospitals now default to this" or "Hospitals increasingly default to this" for accuracy.

### CLAIM 3: Baby-Friendly hospitals eliminate nurseries
**Location:** Line 9, "WHAT IT IS"
**Text:** "Many Baby-Friendly hospitals have eliminated nurseries entirely."

**Status:** ⚠️ PARTIALLY VERIFIED

**Verified Source:**
- **Web research:** Baby-Friendly USA clarification

**Key findings:**
- Baby-Friendly does **not require** nursery closure
- BFUSA Guidelines and Evaluation Criteria require only that **rooming-in be the standard of practice**
- Since late 1990s, 24/7 nursery care has begun to fall out of favor, thanks in part to BFHI
- Some facilities have reduced nursery capacity, but formal closures are not mandated

**Verification notes:** The claim "eliminated nurseries entirely" is stronger than the evidence supports. Many Baby-Friendly hospitals have reduced nursery services but not necessarily eliminated them completely.

**RECOMMENDATION:** Soften to "Some Baby-Friendly hospitals have reduced or eliminated traditional nurseries" or "Baby-Friendly hospitals have de-emphasized nursery use in favor of rooming-in."

**Web sources:**
- [Baby-Friendly USA: Common Misunderstandings](https://www.babyfriendlyusa.org/about/common-misunderstandings/)
- [HuffPost: Behind Baby-Friendly Hospital Practice](https://www.huffpost.com/entry/behind-the-baby-friendly-hospital-practice-that-not-all-moms-love_n_57854bd0e4b08608d332048e)

### CLAIM 5: Proximity triggers bonding hormones
**Location:** Line 15, "THE BENEFITS"
**Text:** "Bonding: Constant proximity triggers bonding hormones"

**Status:** ✅ VERIFIED (General oxytocin research)

**Verified Source:**
- Well-established maternal-infant physiology research (Uvnäs-Moberg and others)

**Verification notes:** Skin-to-skin contact and infant proximity stimulate maternal oxytocin release, which supports bonding behaviors. However, attachment develops through ongoing responsive care rather than initial hours alone. This is foundational knowledge in maternal-infant physiology.

**Note:** While specific DOI not obtained, this claim represents well-established medical knowledge about oxytocin and maternal-infant bonding that does not require controversial sourcing.

### CLAIM 8: Research doesn't account for maternal exhaustion/mental health
**Location:** Line 26, "THE EVIDENCE"
**Text:** "While rooming in supports breastfeeding, studies don't account well for maternal exhaustion and mental health."

**Status:** ✅ VERIFIED (Methodological critique documented)

**Verified Sources:**
Multiple sources document this research gap:

1. **Rooming-in studies typically measure breastfeeding rates but infrequently assess maternal mental health outcomes, sleep quality, or satisfaction**

2. **Methodological weaknesses identified:**
   - Only one RCT included in rooming-in meta-analyses (limits sensitivity analysis)
   - Most maternity care model studies were methodologically weak
   - Lacked reliable and valid tools to gather and analyze data
   - Limited exploration of patient and provider outcomes

3. **Recent research documenting the problem:**
   - Fatigue levels among rooming-in participants significantly high
   - Potential impact of postpartum distress amplified by rooming-in system
   - Association with development/worsening of conditions like hypertension
   - Many women find it challenging to balance recovery with newborn care demands

**Web sources:**
- [Exploring Maternal Challenges in Rooming-in Settings (MDPI)](https://www.mdpi.com/2077-0383/14/1/207)
- [Single-room maternity care systematic review (PMC)](https://pmc.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/articles/PMC7544846/)
- [Experiences of Implementing Rooming-in Practice (PMC)](https://pmc.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/articles/PMC10605654/)

**Verification notes:** This critique is well-supported by recent systematic reviews and research commentaries noting the gap between breastfeeding outcome measurement and maternal well-being assessment.

### CLAIM 9: Brief nursery care doesn't harm outcomes
**Location:** Line 26, "THE EVIDENCE"
**Text:** "A few hours of nursery care so you can sleep has not been shown to harm outcomes."

**Status:** ⚠️ PARTIALLY VERIFIED (Absence of evidence claim)

**Verification notes:** This is a "no evidence of harm" claim, which is difficult to verify definitively. Web research revealed:

- "Despite proposed benefits of full rooming-in, these associations have never been fully substantiated"
- "A recent Cochrane review did not show full supportive evidence for the benefits of full rooming-in on breastfeeding"
- Limited research directly compares continuous rooming-in with intermittent nursery use

The claim as written is defensible but lacks direct citation support. The Cochrane review showing limited evidence for mandatory full rooming-in provides indirect support.

**RECOMMENDATION:** Consider softening to "Limited research suggests that brief nursery care for maternal rest is unlikely to significantly impact bonding or breastfeeding outcomes, though more study is needed."

**Web sources:**
- [Associations of rooming-in with bonding (ScienceDirect)](https://www.sciencedirect.com/science/article/abs/pii/S0266613821000218)
- [WHO ELENA: Rooming-in review](https://www.who.int/tools/elena/review-summaries/early-breastfeeding--rooming-in-for-new-mother-and-infant-versus-separate-care-for-increasing-the-duration-of-breastfeeding)

**Line 9: "Most hospitals now default to this"**
- **Current:** Overstates prevalence
- **Evidence:** Less than half of US hospitals practice rooming-in as default
- **Recommended:** "Many hospitals now default to this" or "Hospitals increasingly default to this"

**Line 9: "Many Baby-Friendly hospitals have eliminated nurseries entirely"**
- **Current:** Stronger than evidence supports
- **Evidence:** BFHI requires rooming-in as standard but doesn't mandate nursery closure
- **Recommended:** "Some Baby-Friendly hospitals have reduced or eliminated traditional nurseries" or "Baby-Friendly hospitals have de-emphasized nursery use"

**Line 26: "A few hours of nursery care so you can sleep has not been shown to harm outcomes"**
- **Current:** Strong absence-of-evidence claim
- **Evidence:** Limited direct research comparing continuous vs. intermittent rooming-in
- **Recommended:** "Limited research suggests that brief nursery care for maternal rest is unlikely to significantly impact bonding or breastfeeding outcomes" or "Brief nursery care for sleep appears compatible with successful breastfeeding and bonding"
