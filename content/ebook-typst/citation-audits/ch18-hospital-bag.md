## VERIFIED CITATIONS REQUIRED

### 1. ⚠️ PARTIALLY VERIFIED: Pacifier Timing Recommendation

**Location:** Line 112
**Claim:** "Pacifier (wait until breastfeeding is established, usually 3-4 weeks)"

**Verification Status:** ⚠️ PARTIALLY VERIFIED

**Evidence Found:**
- Cochrane review on pacifier use and breastfeeding (DOI: 10.1002/14651858.cd007202.pub2)
- AAP policy statements on pacifier use reference SIDS prevention vs. breastfeeding considerations
- The "3-4 weeks" timing is commonly cited in lactation guidance but not definitively established in research

**Recommendation:** MEDIUM PRIORITY - Either:
1. Add citation to AAP breastfeeding policy OR Baby-Friendly Hospital Initiative guidelines
2. Soften language to "typically" or "often recommended" to reflect guidance rather than research-based timing

**Suggested Citation:**
American Academy of Pediatrics Section on Breastfeeding. "Breastfeeding and the Use of Human Milk." *Pediatrics* 2012;129(3):e827-e841.

**Action:** Consider adding citation or softening claim language

**Claims:**

- "Peppermint for nausea"
- "Clary sage to support contractions (use only during active labor)"
- "Frankincense for grounding"

**Verification Status:** MIXED

- ⚠️ **Clary sage for contractions**: LIMITED EVIDENCE - Traditional use but limited research; this is the most problematic claim
- ⚠️ **Frankincense for grounding**: TRADITIONAL USE - Subjective/traditional, not research-based

**Recommendation:** MEDIUM PRIORITY - Either:
1. Cite research for lavender and peppermint; soften clary sage claim
2. Change language to "traditionally used for" rather than stating effects as fact
3. Add citations for the two verified claims

**Suggested Approach:**
"Lavender for relaxation (research-supported), peppermint for nausea (research-supported), clary sage traditionally used during active labor, frankincense for grounding"

**Action:** Consider adding citations for peppermint and lavender, softening clary sage language
