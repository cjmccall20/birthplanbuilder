**Location:** Lines 160-163
**Claim:** Specific essential oils for specific purposes:
- "Lavender for relaxation"
- "Peppermint for nausea"
- "Clary sage to support contractions (use only during active labor)"
- "Frankincense for grounding"

**Category:** Natural remedy claims
**Current Status:** Uncited

**Assessment:**
These are specific therapeutic claims about essential oils. While some of these have research support (particularly peppermint for nausea and lavender for relaxation), the clary sage claim about "supporting contractions" is a medical claim that should be cited or removed.

**Proposed Citation:**
- Lavender for relaxation: Multiple aromatherapy studies in labor settings
- Peppermint for nausea: Research on peppermint aromatherapy for nausea
- Clary sage: Limited research on uterotonic effects (this is the most problematic claim)
- Frankincense: This is more subjective/traditional use

**Recommendation:** MEDIUM-HIGH PRIORITY - Either cite these claims or soften the language to "traditionally used for" rather than stating therapeutic effects as fact.

**Confidence:** Mixed
- Lavender/relaxation: High confidence (well-researched)
- Peppermint/nausea: High confidence (well-researched)
- Clary sage/contractions: Medium confidence (some traditional use, limited research)
- Frankincense/grounding: Low confidence (traditional use, subjective)

**Suggested Sources:**
- Cochrane Review on aromatherapy in labor (if available)
- Individual studies on lavender and peppermint aromatherapy
- Traditional aromatherapy texts for clary sage (or soften claim)
